    • auto retrato - self portrait

    about

    Artist Frida Kahlo’s body of work is outstanding in its ability to reveal what’s behind the flesh of her canvasses. Her evocative and intimate story, admired the world over for its rawness and vulnerability, is a mirror in which Poet Elisabeth Horan sees reflections of her own multi-layered skin as a woman, a lover, and artist whose mind and body sometimes threaten to abandon her when she needs them most.

    In this ekphrastic English/Spanish collection auto retrato - self portrait (English/Italian forthcoming), the worlds of two separate women merge into one – a kaleidoscopic journey of longing, heartbreak, hope and loss that, while others may want to silence, is anything but quiet and submissive.

    Elisabeth Horan - Author

    @ehoranpoet

    Born on the Naval Base in Pensacola, Florida (USA) in 1975, Elisabeth Horan is a renowned American editor, writer, poet and professor. Elisabeth is Honorary Secretary of the International Commission of Writers and was awarded the International Writer’s Award, Geneva, 2014.

    She published Okonkwo Between Two Worlds: Investigation of Masculine/Feminine Binaries in ‘Things Fall Apart’, by Chinua Achebe; The Phallus and the Chalice in ‘The Handmaid’s Tale’, by Margaret Atwood, and Postcolonialism and “The Absolutely True Diary of a Part Time Indian”, investigating the titular non-fiction work by Sherman Alexie.

    Her poetry print publications include: Pensacola Girls, from Bone and Ink Press, Bad Mommy / Stay Mommy, from Fly on the Wall Press, Odd list Odd house Odd me, from Twist It Press, Was It R*pe, from Animal Heart Press, Self-Portrait (Ekphrastic Poetry for Frida Kahlo), from Cephalo Press, Just to the Right of the Stove, from Twist in Time Press, So Righteous We Go Flying, from Animal Heart Press, Alcoholic Betty, from Fly on the Wall Press, Fem Box, from Moonchild Press, and The Mask, from Broken Spine Arts, UK.

    Her poetry awards include Best of the Net Nomination-2019, from An Elephant Never Press, Best of the Net Nomination-2018, from Midnight Lane Boutique and Pushcart Nomination-2018 from Cease Cows.

    She earned her BA in American Literature from Southern Oregon University, MA in English and Creative Writing from Southern New Hampshire University, and her Masters of Fine Arts in Poetry from Lindenwood University.

    Elisabeth teaches English at her local community colleges.

    She is the Editor in Chief at Animal Heart Press, Vermont, USA. She is also Co-Editor at Ice Floe Press, Toronto (Canada), where she selects and curates manuscripts from highly influential and diverse voices from all over the world. She has traveled extensively presenting papers and delivering lectures across countries.

    Elisabeth lives with her husband Joshua Horan on their family farm in Norwich, Vermont, (USA) and is a proud mother to Peter and Thomas Horan.

    Please follow her work @ehoranpoet and at ehoranpoet.net

    Karen Pierce Gonzalez - Publisher

    @folkheartpress

    FolkHeart Press is a boutique publishing company located in the San Francisco Bay Area. Folklore, according to Publisher Karen Pierce Gonzalez, is the street face of mythology; the common person’s interpretation about how his or her world works. FolkHeart Press books, blogs, and special reports highlight the ways folklore continues to do that today. The FolkHeart Press bookstore includes Autoretrato (Self-Portrait), Family Folktales: What Are Yours?, LOVE Potions, Lotions, and Lore, and Spanish Cuisine One Region at a Time: Catalonia.

    A former journalist and folklore columnist who researched scarecrows as a folk art, she is a prose and poetry writer who has written True North (Origami Poems Project) and the forthcoming Coyote in the basket of my ribs (Alabaster Leaves 2023), Down River with Li Po (Black Cat Poetry Review).

    Karen is also a mixed-media assemblage artist who follows the grain of bark, the weave of textiles and the waters of color.

    Marcelle Newbold - Managing editor

    @marcellenewbold

    Marcelle Newbold’s poetry explores place and inheritance. Pushcart Prize nominated, and winner of the Poetry in the Arcades competition in 2020, her poems have been published in online and print magazines including Ink Sweat & Tears, Iamb poet, and The Ekphrastic Review. Her writing has featured in recent print anthologies by Black Bough Poetry, Maytree Press, Wild Pressed Books, Icefloe Press, and Indigo Dreams. She is managing editor of Nightingale & Sparrow literary press, and was coordinator for Gloucester Poetry Festival 2021. Marcelle lives in Cardiff, Wales where she trained as an architect.     Jorge Montero Calderon - Spanish translator

    BBorn in Heredia, province of Costa Rica, under the name of Jorge Eduardo Montero Calderon, my pseudonym is Pablo Heredia, a homage to the province and place of living.

    I love writing Haiku, Landay and other kinds of poetry, one of my books was published in Italy, its name is “Aquihoriedades “a silogism which means that we must live here and now always, every time.

    Translating is one of the things I love most, but my poems has also been published in Spain, Romany, United States, amongst others.

    I am a languages teacher and a therapist, I also love painting murals and working for homeless people, I always avoid judging them and if one thing annoys me is the way people treat them, of course we al know they use drugs, BUT WHY? When I get the answer, the road to their freedoms looks clearer than never before, that is my point.

    Nacido en Heredia, provincia de Costa Rica, bajo el nombre de Jorge Eduardo Montero Calderón, mi seudónimo es Pablo Heredia, un homenaje a la provincia y lugar de vida.

    Me encanta escribir Haiku, Landay y otros tipos de poesía, uno de mis libros fue publicado en Italia, su nombre es “Aquihoriedades”, un silogismo que significa que debemos vivir aquí y ahora siempre, cada vez.

    Traducir es una de las cosas que más me gusta, pero mis poemas también han sido publicados en España, Roma, Estados Unidos, entre otros.

    Soy profesor de idiomas y terapeuta, también escribo para revistas y periódicos de la Provincia de Heredia, me encanta pintar murales y trabajar para las personas sin hogar, siempre evito juzgarlas y si algo me molesta es la forma en que las personas las tratan. Por supuesto que todos sabemos que consumen drogas, PERO ¿POR QUÉ? Cuando obtengo la respuesta, el camino hacia su liberación se ve más claro que nunca, ese es mi punto.

    Valentina Meloni - Italian translator

    Valentina Meloni was born in Rome. Her poetry collections include: Nei Giardini di Suzhou (2015), Le regole del controdolore (2016), Alambic (2018), Nanita (Otata’s Bookshelf, 2017), Eva (2018), and others. She directs and publishes, Komorebi ni nureru Italian Journal, a periodic magazine of Haikai in translation from the English. She writes in literary and cultural magazines, and on her eco-poetry and deep ecology blogs. She lives in Valdichiana and leads a rather retracted life in contact with woods and all the natural beauties that surround and inspire her.
